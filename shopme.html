🐾 wolfie.love How It Works

Platform documentation

Every mechanic of
wolfie.love

A sentence-by-sentence literary memorial for Wolfgang — a rescue dog, a three-year companion, a good boy. This document covers the full reading model: navigation, flow mode, themes, image triggers, chapters, remarks, keyboard shortcuts, and the interstitial dark moment. Consider it the operator's manual for something that isn't quite a website.

01

Navigation

wolfie.love does not scroll. There is no continuous feed. Every move through the piece is a deliberate step — one sentence at a time, forward or backward, at whatever pace the reader chooses. The reading is a series of discrete moments because the grief it describes is too.

Every input method is supported simultaneously. You never have to learn a specific gesture to proceed.

Desktop

Scroll wheel or trackpad advances one sentence per gesture. Arrow keys (↑↓), j/k, and Space also navigate. Clicking any sentence in the stream jumps directly to it. Arrow keys (←→) jump between chapters.

Mobile

Swipe up/down advances or retreats one sentence. Tap anywhere in the stream activates the tapped sentence. The floating HUD (bottom-center) provides prev/next buttons at all times, including in stripped-down reading modes.

Scroll Cooldown

A cooldown prevents accidental double-advances on fast trackpad gestures. The threshold distinguishes intentional swipes from scroll momentum. The active sentence is always scrolled into view automatically — centered in the viewport — regardless of how navigation was triggered.

You don't choose what to skip. You move through the piece at the pace the piece demands. The format is the argument.

02

Sentence Stream

The narrative is parsed at load time from structured source text into discrete units — sentences, chapter breaks, image triggers, theme markers, and interstitial blocks. Each unit becomes a DOM element with its own state, its own role, its own moment.

Active State

The current sentence reads at full opacity with a subtle warm glow. All others dim to 50%. Peripheral sentences remain legible but subordinate — present context, not active focus. Revealed past sentences (already read) maintain a slightly higher opacity than future ones.

Hover (Desktop)

Hovering any non-active sentence raises it — all others dim further to 25%. This lets a reader preview a sentence and surface its associated image without committing to it. Hovering an image trigger shows the photograph without activating the sentence.

Chapter Breaks

Full-width separators with a chapter number and title rendered inline in the stream at the exact sentence where each chapter begins. Clicking a chapter break opens the chapter dropdown. Subchapter titles appear at reduced opacity with a fine accent rule — structural markers, not navigable elements.

Inline Syntax (Author Markers)

@theme:name sets the active theme at a specific point in the narrative. @@dark / @@/dark opens and closes an interstitial darkness block. ####: fires a narrative toast. ##: creates a chapter. #: creates a subchapter. These markers are invisible to the reader — they are instructions to the platform, written in prose.

03

Flow Mode

Flow Mode advances through sentences automatically at a controlled speed. It turns a reading experience into something closer to being read to — the reader surrenders navigation and lets the text arrive. A progress bar across the bottom of the screen shows position through the piece.

Activation

Flow Mode is started via the Flow button in the navigation cluster or the chapter dropdown. Once active, playback controls appear: a play/stop toggle, and slower/faster speed buttons. Any manual navigation gesture — scroll, swipe, keypress — stops Flow Mode immediately and returns control to the reader.

Speed Control

Three speeds are available via the Slower / Faster buttons. The speed label updates live. Slower is meditative — one sentence every several seconds, long enough to re-read. Faster approaches the pace of being read to by someone who reads well. The default is somewhere between.

Theme Persistence

Flow Mode respects the active theme across all sentence transitions. If you've switched to Memory or Nightfall, the platform holds that theme through every advance — it does not reset. Content-authored @theme: markers still trigger when their sentence is reached, overriding the active theme within their scope. When you navigate back out of a themed section, the previously active theme is restored.

Image Triggers in Flow

When Flow Mode reaches a sentence that contains an image trigger, the associated photograph appears automatically and fades out when the sentence changes. You don't have to hover. The images arrive with the sentences, as if the piece is showing you what it wants you to see.

The Interstitial Dark in Flow

Flow Mode does not skip or accelerate through the interstitial dark block. When the piece goes dark, Flow Mode pauses. The platform waits in the dark for the reader to press forward. This is not a timeout. It holds until you're ready. Then it continues.

04

Themes

Four themes, each a complete visual identity — typeface pairings, color systems, texture character, and ambient mood. They are not skins. Reading the same sentence in Newspaper vs. Memory is a different experience of that sentence. Context is meaning.

Cycle manually via T or the mode cluster. Content can trigger themes automatically via @theme: markers embedded in the narrative, shifting the palette as the emotional register shifts.

📖 Newspaper

Warm off-white. Near-black ink. Saddle brown accent. Reads like something printed and kept. The default of the piece.

🌙 Nightfall

Deep indigo-black. Softer blue-white text. The most interior, most quiet of the four. The reading-alone-at-2am theme.

⚡ Sport

Deep charcoal. Fire orange accent. High contrast, urgent. The piece reads like a brief or a dispatch.

▓ Memory

Warm dark brown. Amber tones. Soft grain. The most personal and tactile of the four — like a wallet photograph.

Transitions

Theme changes include a brief cross-fade with a theme-transitioning class that prevents harsh palette flashes. The transition is fast enough to feel immediate but smooth enough not to jar. The grain and texture overlays transition with the palette.

Content-Driven Themes

The narrative can trigger theme changes automatically using @theme:name markers. When a sentence with a theme marker is reached during reading or Flow Mode, the theme shifts. Navigating backward past a theme marker restores the theme that preceded it — the platform scans back to find the nearest active marker for any given position.

05

Image Layer

Photographs of Wolfie are bound to specific words and phrases in the text. They exist in a separate layer behind the prose, surfaced associatively through trigger words rather than dropped into the reading flow as blocks. The images are evidence. They make the dog real.

Image Triggers

Phrases bound to photographs are rendered as underlined trigger spans within the sentence text. Hovering (desktop) or tapping (mobile) a trigger surfaces the associated photograph. The image appears behind the text as a full-viewport presence. When the sentence changes, the image fades.

Auto-Surface on Active Sentence

When a sentence containing an image trigger becomes active, the first image in that sentence is shown automatically — no hover required. The photograph arrives with the sentence, as if the prose and the image are arriving together.

Split Layout — Right Rail

In Split View, the image layer is pinned to the right half of the screen at a fixed position rather than flooding the viewport. Text occupies the left. Reading and looking happen side by side without overlap.

Mobile Image Behavior

On mobile, images appear as a floating panel above the text rather than as a full-viewport background — preserving readability while keeping the photograph present. Tapping an image trigger surfaces it; navigating away fades it.

06

The Dark Moment

At a specific point in the narrative — now we go sleepy — the interface disappears entirely. Not dimmed. Dark. The keyboard legend, chapter bar, mode cluster, nav buttons, progress indicators — all of it is gone. There is only the sentence, and then nothing.

How It Works

The author marks an @@dark block in the source text. When the reader reaches the first sentence inside that block, the platform snapshots the current theme, applies interstitial-dark to the body, and hides every navigational element via CSS. A full-screen overlay takes the viewport.

The Wait

The platform does not auto-advance. There is no timeout. It holds in the dark until the reader presses forward. This is intentional. The platform has an opinion about how long this moment takes, and the opinion is: however long it takes.

Theme Restore

When the reader navigates out of the dark block — forward past the @@/dark marker, or backward out of it — the snapshotted theme is restored. The world reassembles. Navigation controls return. The piece continues.

Per-Theme Styling

Each theme defines its own interstitial-dark palette — the darkness is theme-aware. Newspaper's dark is warmer; Nightfall's is deeper; Sport's is higher contrast. The darkness is not generic. It fits the world the reader was already in.

The interface earns its own disappearance. You are not losing the controls — you are losing the need for them. That is the design.

07

Chapters

The narrative is divided into chapters, each beginning at a defined sentence. Chapters provide structure and jump targets. Subchapters title sections within chapters — they are editorial, not navigational.

Chapter Dropdown

Opened via the brand logo (top-left), the C key, or clicking any chapter break in the stream. Lists all chapters. The active chapter is highlighted. Clicking any item jumps immediately to that chapter's first sentence. The dropdown is draggable — drag by the header to reposition it anywhere on screen. Position persists across open/close cycles for the session.

Chapter Progress Bar

A thin bar above the content shows progress within the current chapter — not the piece overall. The chapter name updates as you navigate. Chapter-level progress is more meaningful than piece-level progress in a work structured as discrete emotional sections.

Chapter Tracking

The platform tracks which chapters have been entered. This state is used by the toast system and by the stats widget's chapter counter. A chapter is considered visited when its first sentence is activated.

08

Visitor Remarks

Visitor Remarks are short annotations — up to 80 characters — that readers can leave anchored to specific sentences in the piece. They are the platform's only social layer: shared, persistent, and attached to the text itself rather than floating in a comment section beneath it.

Leaving a Remark

When a sentence is active, a remark input appears anchored below it. Type a response to that sentence — what it made you think of, a memory it surfaced, a word that fits. Submit and the remark is saved to the sentence, visible to all subsequent readers at that position.

Surfacing Remarks

When a reader reaches a sentence that has remarks attached, the remarks surface as a subtle notification. Remarks from other visitors appear inline below the sentence text — not in a sidebar, not at the bottom of the page. They are part of the reading, anchored exactly where they belong.

Storage

Remarks are stored via window.storage with shared: true — they are globally shared across all readers. The remark is stored with its anchored sentence index and text, and loaded on each visit. Once per session, the platform checks whether new remarks have appeared at the reader's current position.

Character Limit

80 characters. This is enforced at the input. The limit is not punitive — it is editorial. A remark at this length is a reaction, not an essay. It is the size of a thought you'd write in the margin of a book you loved.

Keyboard Shortcuts

The full keyboard map. All shortcuts are single-key unless noted. Case-insensitive.

10

View Modes

Each mode is a different reading contract. Not settings — states of attention.

Default (Layered)

Full experience. All chrome visible. Images appear behind the text as full-viewport presences triggered by sentence activation or hover. The intended reading state for a first encounter with the piece.

Split View — L

Text occupies the left half. Imagery is pinned to the right at a fixed position. Reading and looking happen side by side without overlap. Requires landscape orientation on mobile — a rotate prompt appears if needed.

Read Mode — H

All chrome hidden. The piece takes the full viewport. Navigation buttons ghost to very low opacity but remain interactive. Background images still surface. On mobile: longpress (300ms) on the stream triggers this. A top-right hotspot restores the UI when touched.

Focus Mode — F

Chrome hidden. Background image layer suppressed. Only the text and the active sentence. The purest reading state — nothing between the reader and the words. Combine with any theme for a different character of quiet.

Mode Persistence

Modes are not mutually exclusive with themes — Focus Mode in Nightfall is different from Focus Mode in Newspaper. The mode cluster widget reflects active state. In Read Mode and Focus Mode, a top-right viewport hotspot reveals a close cursor on hover — clicking restores full UI without requiring a keyboard.

11

Mobile

Mobile is a fully supported first-class reading experience with its own interaction model — not a degraded desktop view. The gesture vocabulary is native, and the emotional mechanics are fully preserved at pocket scale.

Swipe Navigation

Swipe up to advance, swipe down to retreat. The gesture maps to turning through something — not pages exactly, but moments. The threshold distinguishes a swipe from a scroll, and a tap from a gesture. A cooldown prevents accidental double-advances.

HUD Widget

A floating bar centered above the bottom safe area. Contains prev/next navigation buttons flanking a sentence counter. In Read Mode the widget ghosts low but the nav buttons remain fully interactive at full opacity. The HUD is the one piece of chrome that never fully disappears.

Longpress → Read Mode

A 300ms longpress anywhere on the stream hides all UI chrome. This is close enough to a normal tap to feel natural but deliberate enough not to fire accidentally. A subtle vibration confirms the gesture on supported devices. All swipe navigation works normally while in Read Mode.

Image Display

On mobile, images appear as a floating panel above the text layer rather than as full-viewport backgrounds — preserving readability while keeping the photograph present and large enough to land. Image triggers in the text are tap targets.

Mobile Mode Cluster

The mode cluster is visible on mobile but reduced — Focus Mode is desktop-only (cursor-dependent), Read Mode is handled by longpress. Theme, Layout, and Chapter remain accessible. No drag handle on mobile.

12

Narrative Toasts

Toasts are the platform's passive voice — messages that appear contextually during reading, attached to specific moments or behaviors. Two kinds: system toasts that orient the reader to the platform's mechanics, and narrative toasts authored directly into the piece.

Narrative Toasts (####:)

The author can embed a toast message directly into any sentence using the ####: message text marker. When the reader reaches that sentence, the toast appears as a banner above the stream. These fire every time that sentence is reached — they are part of the piece, not a one-time onboarding notice. They are the piece speaking alongside itself.

Welcome Banners

A pool of welcome banners surfaces on first load, after a short delay. Each highlights a different mechanic: navigation, flow mode, themes, image triggers, keyboard shortcuts. They orient without interrupting — they arrive once, and do not repeat in the same session.

Idle Detection

If no navigation occurs for 15 seconds, a gentle prompt appears. Different wording for mobile (swipe hint) and desktop (spacebar hint). It asks without demanding. It disappears the moment you move.

Toast Stack

Up to two standard toasts stack at the bottom-left. Banner toasts (narrative and welcome) appear centered below the header, one at a time, and supersede the stack. The aria-live="polite" toast container ensures screen readers receive toast content without interrupting active reading.
