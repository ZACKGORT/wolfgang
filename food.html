On Tap Now Furnace

Gold. Our flagship. A golden ale brewed with Cascade and Centennial hops, dry-hopped for four days before it touches the tap. Honey and stone fruit on the nose. Finishes clean and fast. This is the one you order when you don't know what to order. The Dark Foundry Stout has been on tap every winter since we opened. Roasted barley, oat adjunct, a long cold conditioning in the cellar. It pours black with a tan head and tastes like coffee you'd actually pay for. 6.8% ABV. Not a meal, but close. We rotate eight seasonal handles. Right now: a tart cherry wheat from last summer's harvest, a hazy IPA we're calling Copperhead that got out of hand in the best way, and a pilsner so clean it makes you suspicious. The board above the bar is always current. The Kitchen 03 What We Cook Honest food · sourced close · made here
The Kitchen The pretzel is the thing. Two feet long, baked in the wood-fired oven out back, served with a whole-grain mustard we make in-house and a beer cheese that uses whatever's on tap that week. It sounds simple. People order a second one. The smash burger is beef from Halcyon Farm, twenty miles east. Two patties, smashed on the flat top, American cheese, house pickles, a sauce we've never written down. It comes with fries that are thicker than they should be and better for it. We serve it until the kitchen closes. The Cellar 04 Aged & Reserved Barrel program · limited bottles · cellar releases
The Barrel Program We have forty-eight barrels in the cellar. Bourbon barrels from a distillery in Kentucky, wine barrels from the valley, a handful of rye whiskey casks we got from a friend. Everything down there is patient. Nothing comes up until it's ready. "The cellar doesn't care about your timeline. Neither do we when something's down there."
