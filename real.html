Part Two 02 How They Met Portland, Oregon · September 2019
Portland, Oregon · September 2019 It was a bookshop. Not a metaphor — an actual bookshop on NW 23rd, a Tuesday afternoon in September when it was raining the way Portland rains in September, which is without apology. Eleanor was looking for something she'd read half of on a flight and abandoned. James was working the register. She couldn't remember the title. She remembered the cover — blue, she thought, or maybe grey. James spent twenty minutes with her. They found it. It was neither blue nor grey. It was the wrong book entirely, and she bought it anyway. "I read the whole thing on the bus home and it was terrible. I went back the next day to tell him." He was working again. She told him it was terrible. He agreed — he'd read it — and showed her three better options. They talked for an hour. She bought all three. She has still only read one of them. It doesn't matter. That's not why she went back.
Part Three 03 The Proposal Montepulciano, Italy · August 2023
Montepulciano, Italy · August 2023 James had been carrying the ring for eleven days. Through Florence, Siena, two train delays, a lost reservation, and a dinner they'd have to remember forever. He had planned to ask at the vineyard. It rained. He had planned to ask at the piazza. There was a wedding already happening — some other couple's, noisy and perfect and entirely in the way. He asked on the walk back to the hotel. No view, no audience. A narrow street, eleven at night, a single lamp above a shuttered door. She said yes before he finished asking. She still won't let him finish the question when he tries to tell the story. "I knew what he was going to say. I'd known for a while. I just wanted him to know I already knew." The night after · still wearing yesterday's clothes
Part Four 04 The Venue Stonegate Farm · Hudson Valley, NY
Stonegate Farm · Hudson Valley, New York Stonegate Farm sits on 80 acres above the Hudson, two hours from the city and a complete other world from it. The ceremony is in the meadow — open sky, the Catskills in the background doing what mountains do when everything else is important. The reception is in the barn, restored and reimagined, lit by a thousand points of light and the logic of one very good party. Eleanor found it on a Tuesday in February, walking through the website at midnight, and called James at 12:08 to say she'd found it. He was asleep. He said yes without fully waking up. In the morning she asked if he remembered. He said yes. He had no idea what he'd agreed to, and it didn't matter at all.
