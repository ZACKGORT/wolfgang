New York · Chapter Four 04 FlightPath PHI → NYC · Pharma · WCAG · The daily commute
PHI → NYC Hello,
New York. FlightPath — recently emerged from bankruptcy, drastically downsized, preserving vital pharma client relationships. The pay was passable at best. But this was my foot-in-the-door to the NYC agency scene, and I knew it. FlightPath was always only ever my runway. Commuting from Philly to Manhattan every business day for a full year. Up and out by five; home by ten, maybe. WCAG 2.0 AA and AAA compliance, legal and regulatory marketing scrutiny, pharmaceutical clients demanding precision in every pixel. "Two skylines. Three hours apart. Saw both sunrise and sunset every day. Worth it? Totally." The real education here was constraint. Pharma UX is the most regulated design environment on the planet. Legal approves copy. Medical approves claims. Compliance approves layout. You learn to be exact. You learn that every pixel is a decision, not a default. RoleLead UX Designer · Midtown Manhattan ClientsMerck Animal Health · Zoetis · Charter · Goya · The Dana Foundation · 2018–20
New York · Chapter Five 05 LiveArea IC → Manager · B2B & B2C e-commerce · Cards Against Bad UX
From IC to Manager Live.
Area. LiveArea — from IC to Manager. I owned the UX process for a gamut of B2B & B2C e-commerce brands via Salesforce Commerce Cloud. UX Manager on contracts exceeding $1.2M. Billable to the client at $235/hr. Everything I did had a direct impact on the deliverable. I ran discovery. I directed and delegated. I socialized my solutions. Then: several rounds of client frustration with unfamiliar process. So I built Cards Against Bad UX — a card game played in workshops to humanize the user, depoliticize the design conversation, and shift the table from features to feelings. "Clients don’t always know what they want. They always know how something makes them feel." Cards Against Bad UX wasn’t a side project. It was a direct response to the friction I observed at the workshop table. Jargon-laden spreadsheets and single-sided meetings were failing everyone. A card game changed the room temperature. It worked. RoleUX Manager · Salesforce Commerce Cloud · $1.2M+ contracts ClientsYETI · West Marine · Aquasana · Hardinge · Simpson Strong-Tie · 2020–21

New York · Chapter Nine 09 Publicis Sapient BNY Mellon · Pershing X · Wove · De facto Team Lead
375 Hudson Street I was
not one. I became one. Publicis Sapient — 375 Hudson Street, “Agency Row.” A full year embedded in Financial Services, serving BNY Mellon’s Pershing X Advisory Experience Platform. Exhaustive research. Client workshops. Design systems. Data refactorization for a suite of Financial Advisor applications. The problem: advisors worked across many disconnected tools. Context was lost. Data had to be re-entered at every handoff. We sought to flatten the experience through a uniform, interconnected dashboard — Wove — an industry-first platform that connects a client’s full financial picture with the tools advisors actually use. "Information architecture, task and flow mapping, cross-module navigation, and component patterns that keep data alive across steps — no reentry." My contract stated full-time remote. I opted to be a consistent in-office presence anyway. I became the de facto Team Lead and Product Owner. I led research, discovery, prototype development, and testing — across multiple concurrent tracks. I mentored junior and associate designers. I set my team up for success daily. This is important context: this was only 2023 into early 2024, before the AI buzzword craze. Zero artificial intelligence was involved. It was human blood, sweat, and copious amounts of caffeine that analyzed every legacy application, mapped the flows, socialized it with the team, and ultimately shipped a viable, modernized financial product ecosystem to one of the largest banking entities in the world.
